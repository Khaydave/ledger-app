Privacy Policy

Ledger — last updated July 13, 2026

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how Ledger ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects information when you use the Ledger app to track stock, sales, orders, and customer debts for your business. By creating a shop on Ledger, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

2. Information We Collect

Account information

  • Your shop code and password (your password is never stored in plain text — it is hashed before being saved).
  • A security question and answer, used only to help you recover your account if you forget your password.

Profile information (optional, provided by you)

  • Your name, shop name, business location, phone number (with country code), email address, and a profile photo.

Business data you create

  • Stock items, prices, and quantities; sales records; customer debts; pending orders; expenses; and your restock and catalogue lists.

Preferences

  • Your chosen currency, display language, shop type (Regular seller or Dropshipper), and light/dark appearance setting.

Automatically collected information

  • Basic usage information such as login timestamps, used to keep your account secure and to understand overall app usage.

3. How We Use Your Information

  • To operate your shop's core features — stock, sales, debts, orders, expenses, and reporting.
  • To let you optionally generate a public catalogue page that customers can view without an account.
  • To convert amounts between currencies using your selected currency.
  • To help you recover access to your account if you forget your password.
  • To maintain, secure, and improve Ledger over time.

We do not sell your personal information to third parties, and we do not use your data for advertising.

4. The Public Catalogue Feature — What Becomes Public

This section matters: unlike the rest of your Ledger account, information you choose to add to your public catalogue is intentionally visible to anyone with the link — no login required.

If you choose to build and share a public catalogue link, the following information becomes visible to anyone who opens that link: your shop name, profile photo, location, the items and prices you have specifically selected to include, any caption you add, and — if provided — your phone number, used to generate a WhatsApp contact link for customers. You control exactly which stock items are included, and you can update or remove them at any time.

5. How We Store and Protect Your Information

Your data is stored using Google Firebase, a cloud infrastructure provider. We take reasonable steps to protect your information, including password hashing and standard cloud security practices. However, no method of online storage or transmission is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. You are responsible for keeping your shop code and password confidential.

6. Data Sharing with Third Parties

We do not sell or rent your personal data. We use the following third-party services to operate Ledger:

  • Google Firebase — for secure data storage and hosting.
  • WhatsApp — only when you choose to use the "Message on WhatsApp" feature on your public catalogue, which opens a conversation using the phone number you provided.

7. Your Rights

Depending on applicable law, including the Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) 2023, you may have the right to access, correct, or delete your personal information, and to withdraw consent for its processing. To exercise these rights, contact us using the details in Section 11.

8. Data Retention

We retain your account information for as long as your shop account remains active. If you would like your account and associated data deleted, you can do this yourself at any time from Settings → Delete account, or contact us and we will process the request within a reasonable time.

9. Children's Privacy

Ledger is intended for business owners and is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect information from anyone under the age of 18.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time as Ledger evolves. We will update the "last updated" date at the top of this page when changes are made.
